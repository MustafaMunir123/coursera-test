Main Parts Of Seed

Embryo

An embryo is a portion of a seed that grows into a plant. After the proper conditions for germination (e.g., light, moisture, temperature, oxygen) are met the tiny plant embryo germinates and sprouts into a mature seedling.

Cotyledon

A leaf of the embryo of a seed-bearing plant.Cotyledons either store food for the growing embryo or absorb food that has been stored in the endosperm for eventual distribution to the growing parts of the embryo. Also called seed leaf.

Seed Coat

The seed coat is one of three parts of a seed, in addition to the endosperm, or the food storage area of the seed, as well as the plant embryo. Seed coats on alfalfa, radish, while the coats on corn, peas, and squash tend to be light.
